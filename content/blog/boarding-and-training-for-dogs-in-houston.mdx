## What is Board and Training for Dogs?

Boarding and training for dogs is a comprehensive program where your dog stays with us for a specified period while undergoing intensive training. This setup allows our trainers to work closely with your dog, providing consistent and effective training in a controlled environment.

During their stay, dogs receive personalized attention and a structured routine that promotes learning and behavior modification. This approach ensures that your dog not only learns new behaviors but also unlearns any negative habits they may have picked up.

## Cons of Sending Your Dog to a Boarding and Training

While board and training for dogs offer numerous benefits, it’s important to consider some potential drawbacks. Here are a few cons to keep in mind:

### ● Limited Owner Involvement

One of the main disadvantages of a boarding and training program is the limited involvement of the dog owner. Since the dog stays at the boarding and training facility, owners might miss out on learning how to handle their dog’s behavior issues directly. This lack of hands-on experience can make it challenging to maintain the new behaviors once the dog returns home.

### ● Stress and Anxiety

Being in a new environment can be stressful for some dogs. Separation anxiety, in particular, can be exacerbated when a dog is away from its familiar home environment for an extended period. Even in a good boarding and training facility, the change in routine and the presence of other dogs and new people can cause anxiety.

### ● Temporary Results

Without ongoing support and follow-up training sessions, some dogs may revert to their old behaviors once they return home. This is particularly true if the dog owners do not continue training and reinforce the new behaviors consistently.

### ● Cost

Boarding and training programs can be expensive. The comprehensive training, boarding, and care provided by certified professional dog trainers come at a premium price, which may not be affordable for all dog owners.

### ● Risk of Inconsistent Training Techniques

Not all boarding and training facilities use the same training techniques. Some may use punishment-based techniques, which can have negative effects on a dog’s behavior and well-being. It’s crucial to ensure the facility employs positive reinforcement methods to promote good behavior and build a positive relationship between the dog and the trainer.

## Boarding and Training for Puppies

Boarding and training for young puppies can be incredibly beneficial, as it sets the foundation for good behavior and proper socialization early on. Here’s what you can expect from a puppy class at a board and train facility:

### I. Early Socialization

Puppies enrolled in a boarding and training program have the opportunity to socialize with other dogs, people, and different environments. This early exposure is crucial for developing a well-rounded and confident dog.

### II. Basic Obedience

Puppies will learn essential obedience commands such as sit, stay, come, and leash walking. These basic commands are the building blocks of a well-behaved dog and are easier to teach at a young age.

### III. Potty Training

Potty training is a critical aspect of a puppy’s training. A good board and train program will include consistent routines and schedules to help puppies learn where and when to eliminate.

### IV. Bite Inhibition

Puppies naturally explore the world with their mouths, which can lead to [nipping and biting](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/nip#:~:text=%3A%20to%20bite%20or%20pinch%20someone,wind%20nipped%20at%20my%20nose.). Trainers in a board and train facility will teach puppies bite inhibition, ensuring they learn to control the pressure of their bites.

###  V. Reducing Anxiety

Training in a new environment can help puppies become more adaptable and less anxious. By experiencing different environments and routines, puppies learn to cope with changes and become more resilient.

## How to Choose a Boarding and Training Program for Your Dog

Selecting the right boarding and training program for your dog is crucial to ensure effective training and a positive experience. Here are some key factors to consider:

### ● Research and Reviews

Start by researching boarding and training programs in your area. Look for reviews and testimonials from other dog owners to gauge the reputation and effectiveness of the program.

### ● Training Techniques

Ensure that the facility uses positive reinforcement training techniques rather than punishment-based methods. Positive reinforcement promotes good behavior and strengthens the bond between the dog and the trainer.

### ● Facility Tour

Visit the boarding and training facility to see the environment where your dog will stay. Check for cleanliness, safety measures, and the overall condition of the facility. A good board and train facility should be well-maintained and provide a comfortable environment for the dogs.

### ● Trainer Qualifications

Verify the qualifications and experience of the trainers. Certified professional dog trainers should have the necessary credentials and a proven track record of successful training outcomes.

### ● Customized Training Plans

Ensure that the program offers customized training plans tailored to your dog’s specific needs and behavior issues. A one-size-fits-all approach is less likely to be effective for addressing individual challenges.

### ● Support

Check if the program includes follow-up support and private training sessions after the initial boarding and training period. Ongoing support is essential to reinforce new behaviors and address any potential issues that may arise after your dog returns home.

### ● Cost and Packages

Consider the cost of the program and what is included in the package. While boarding and training programs can be expensive, it’s important to understand the value you are receiving in terms of training quality and comprehensive care.

### ● Compatibility with Your Dog

Finally, ensure that the boarding and training program is a good fit for your dog’s personality and behavior. Some dogs may thrive in a different environment, while others might benefit more from private sessions or group classes. Discuss your dog’s specific needs with the trainers to determine the best approach.

Thoroughly evaluating these factors helps you choose a boarding and training program that will provide the best training experience for your dog, leading to improved behavior and a happier, healthier life together.
