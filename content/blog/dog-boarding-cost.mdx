Dog owners know that their furry friends are more than just pets. They’re part of the family. But what happens when you need to travel or be away from home? Dog boarding services offer a solution. They provide a safe and comfortable environment for your dog while you’re away. But how much does dog boarding cost?

However, the cost of these services can vary greatly. Factors such as location, duration of stay, and the size of your dog can all influence the price.

In this article, we’ll delve into the factors that influence dog boarding costs. We’ll also provide insights into average costs and offer tips on finding the best value for your money.

## Understanding Dog Boarding

[Dog boarding is a service](https://www.thedoghouseps.com/day-care/) that takes care of your dog in your absence. It’s like a hotel for dogs.

These facilities [provide food](https://tfd.partners/thedoghousepetsalon), shelter, and care for your pet. Some even offer additional services like grooming and training.

Boarding can be a great option if you’re traveling or unable to care for your dog temporarily. It ensures your pet is safe and well-cared for.

However, it’s important to choose the right facility. The quality of care can vary greatly between different boarding services.

Understanding what influences dog boarding cost can help you make an informed decision.

## Average Dog Boarding Costs

The cost of dog boarding can vary widely. It depends on several factors, which we’ll discuss in detail later.

On average, you can expect to pay between $25 to $45 per night for a basic kennel. This price typically includes food, shelter, and basic care.

For luxury dog hotels or specialized services, the cost can go up significantly. These facilities can charge anywhere from $50 to $150 per night.

Here’s a rough breakdown of average costs:

- Basic kennel: $25 – $45 per night
- Mid-range facility: $50 – $75 per night
- Luxury dog hotel: $80 – $150 per night

Remember, these are just averages. The actual cost can vary based on your location, the size of your dog, and the specific services you choose.

## 4 Factors That Affect Dog Boarding Costs

Several factors can influence the cost of dog boarding. Understanding these factors can help you budget for this expense.

The location of the boarding facility is a major factor. Prices can vary greatly from city to city, and even within the same city.

The type of facility also plays a role. Luxury dog hotels or specialized boarding schools will cost more than basic kennels.

The duration of your dog’s stay and the size of your dog can also affect the cost. Longer stays and larger dogs typically cost more.

Peak seasons and holidays can lead to increased prices. This is due to higher demand for boarding services during these times.

Finally, any additional services or special care your dog needs can add to the cost. This includes things like grooming, one-on-one playtime, or medical care.

### 1. Location and Facility Type

The location of the boarding facility can greatly affect the cost. Facilities in larger cities or high-cost areas typically charge more.

The type of facility also plays a big role. Basic kennels are usually the most affordable option.

Mid-range facilities may offer more amenities and services, leading to higher costs.

Luxury dog hotels often provide the highest level of care and comfort, but at a premium price.

[Specialized boarding schools or training programs](https://www.thedoghouseps.com/grooming-school/) can also be more expensive due to the additional services they provide.

![](https://www.thedoghouseps.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/03/image-25.png)

### 2. Duration of Stay and Size of the Dog

The length of your dog’s stay is a straightforward factor. The longer the stay, the higher the cost.

However, some facilities may offer discounts for extended stays. It’s worth asking about this if your dog will be staying for a week or more.

The size of your dog can also affect the cost. Larger dogs usually cost more to board.

This is because they require more food and space, and may require more care and attention.

However, not all facilities charge based on size, so it’s important to ask about this when comparing prices.

### 3. Peak Seasons and Holidays

Peak seasons and holidays can lead to increased prices for dog boarding. This is due to higher demand for services during these times.

For example, you may find that prices are higher during the summer months or around major holidays.

It’s a good idea to book well in advance during these times to secure a spot and potentially better rates.

### 4. Additional Services and Special Care

Additional services can add to the cost of dog boarding. These can include things like grooming, one-on-one playtime, or special medical care.

For example, if your dog requires medication or has a special diet, there may be an additional fee.

Some facilities offer package deals that include these services. This can be a cost-effective option if your dog requires multiple additional services.

It’s important to ask about these fees upfront so you can budget accordingly.

The best facility for your dog is not necessarily the cheapest one. It’s the one that can provide the care and services your dog needs.

## Dog Boarding School and Training Costs

Dog boarding schools offer more than just a place for your dog to stay. They provide training programs that can help with behavior issues or teach new skills.

These programs can be quite beneficial. However, they do come at a higher cost than standard boarding.

The cost can vary based on the length and intensity of the program. It also depends on the specific skills being taught.

Despite the higher cost, many owners find these programs to be a good investment. They can lead to better behavior and a happier, more balanced dog in the long run.
