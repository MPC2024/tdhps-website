## Why Nail Care Matters for Dogs

Dogs rely on their paws for nearly every activity, from walking and running to playing and exploring. Overgrown nails can lead to many issues, including pain, mobility challenges, and injuries. ## The Health Benefits of Regular Nail Maintenance

### Healthy Paws, Happy Life

Untrimmed nails can cause significant discomfort for dogs. Long nails push against the ground, creating pressure on the nail bed and potentially causing pain in the toes and joints. Over time, this can lead to arthritis or other joint-related problems. Regular nail trimming ensures your dog can walk and run without strain or discomfort.

Beyond mobility, trimmed nails prevent painful [splits and infections](https://www.msdvetmanual.com/dog-owners/skin-disorders-of-dogs/pyoderma-in-dogs). A visit to The Dog House Pet Salon for **regular nail filing** can help you avoid these complications and ensure your dog’s nails are always in optimal condition.

Proper nail care complements other aspects of grooming, such as bathing. A clean, well-groomed dog is less prone to skin irritations or infections. ## Safety and Injury Prevention Through Nail Care

### Preventing Snags and Tears

Overgrown nails are more than just a nuisance; they’re a safety hazard. Long nails can easily snag on carpets, furniture, or other surfaces, leading to painful breaks or tears. In severe cases, these injuries can require medical attention and prolonged healing periods.

Maintaining your dog’s nails at an appropriate length significantly reduces the risk of such injuries.
