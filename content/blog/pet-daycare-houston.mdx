## FAQS

### · How much does it cost to board a dog in Houston?

Additional or personalized care can be more.

### · Do dogs like going to doggy daycare?

Most dogs enjoy going to doggy daycare, especially if they are social and energetic. Daycare provides an opportunity for dogs to interact with other dogs, get regular exercise, and break up the monotony of being home alone. Dogs that thrive in social environments tend to love daycare, as it allows them to play, explore, and be mentally stimulated. However, not every dog will enjoy daycare. Some dogs may be more anxious or introverted and may prefer a quieter environment. It’s important to evaluate your dog’s temperament to ensure daycare is a good fit.

### · How many days a week should a dog go to daycare?

The ideal number of days a week a dog should go to daycare depends on the dog’s energy levels, personality, and individual needs. For high-energy dogs, **2 to 3 days per week** is often recommended to provide sufficient exercise and mental stimulation without overwhelming them. For older or more sedentary dogs, **1 day per week** may be enough to prevent boredom and allow for socialization. It’s also important to monitor your dog’s behavior to ensure they aren’t becoming overstimulated or stressed.

### . Do dogs age out of daycare?

Dogs generally do not “age out” of daycare, but their needs and preferences may change as they get older. Senior dogs may require less physical activity and more rest, which some dog daycares accommodate with separate areas or quieter playgroups for older dogs. As dogs age, they may become less social or develop medical conditions that make daycare less suitable. It’s essential to find a daycare facility that can tailor activities and care based on your dog’s age and health.

### · Is it stressful for dogs to go to daycare?

While many dogs enjoy daycare, it can be stressful for some, especially during the initial visits. Dogs that are anxious, shy, or have difficulty adapting to new environments may find daycare overwhelming. The unfamiliar environment, the presence of other dogs, and the separation from their owners can all contribute to stress. However, many dogs adjust after a few visits, especially when introduced to daycare gradually. Choosing a daycare with experienced staff and a structured environment can help reduce stress.

### · Do dogs get sad when you leave them at daycare?

Some dogs may experience temporary separation anxiety when dropped off at daycare, especially if they are particularly attached to their owners. Signs of sadness can include whining, pacing, or looking for their owner. However, many dogs quickly adapt once they begin interacting with other dogs or engaging in activities. Over time, most dogs come to associate daycare with positive experiences, such as playtime and socialization, and will become excited when they arrive.

### · What age is best for dog daycare?

Puppies as young as **12 to 16 weeks** can start attending daycare, provided they have completed their basic vaccinations, including those for rabies, distemper, and parvovirus. Early exposure to daycare can be beneficial for socializing puppies, helping them develop good behavior and confidence around other dogs. However, it’s important to ensure that the daycare facility separates puppies from older, more rambunctious dogs to prevent any accidents or overwhelming situations.
