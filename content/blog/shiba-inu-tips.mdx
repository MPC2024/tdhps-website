Here are more interesting facts & characteristics of the Shiba Inu: 

### 1) Shiba Inus Shed Heavily

With[Shiba Inus](https://dogtime.com/dog-breeds/shiba-inu#/slide/1), expect regular grooming and fur around the house. The thick double coat of a Shiba sure is gorgeous—but it will bring a lot of fur into your life. 

The outer layer is stiff and straight, while the inner one is soft and thick. Shibas shed throughout the year, and their plush undercoat completely “blows” twice a year. 

During the period when the shedding amps up, their [hair](https://www.thedoghouseps.com/guide-keeping-dog-hair-under-control/) will go pretty much everywhere. 

Thankfully, lint rollers and vacuums exist. But, if you’re an extreme neat freak, you may want to consider a breed with a thinner coat. 

Or, you can schedule frequent pet grooming Houston [trips to the salon](https://www.thedoghouseps.com/pet-puppies-or-dogs-new-to-grooming/) to keep your pup’s fur under control. 

### 2) The Shiba Inu is an Ancient Dog Breed from Japan

DNA analysis confirms that the Shiba Inu is the oldest and smallest dog breeds from Japan. Believe it or not, ancient ancestors of the Shiba Inus were thought to have accompanied the earliest settlers to Japan as early as 7000 BC.

So, this small, compact, and agile pup were originally bred as a hunting dog designed to navigate and flush small game and birds of the dense underbrush of Japanese forests.

### 3) There Used to Be Six Kinds of Shibas

The Shiba Inu breed is one of the six distinct original native Japanese breeds.

These Japanese breeds from smallest to largest include Shiba Inu, Shikoku, Kishu, Kai, Hokkaido, Akita.

Out of these six breeds, the Shiba Inu is recognized in Japan and throughout the world. In Japan, the Shiba Inu is also known as the official national dog of Japan, and the breed was even declared as a national treasure in 1936.

Though each of the original breeds is different, each still shares similar traits because of their shared ancestry.

### 4) They Have the Personality of a Ninja Warrior

Like a ninja warrior, the Shiba Inu moves quickly, nimbly, and effortlessly. A fierce guardian of possessions, Shiba Inus, are often independent and courageous and bold in character.

However, these dominant [personality traits](http://www.pethealthnetwork.com/dog-health/dog-breeds/shiba-inu) can quickly turn into aggression if left unmonitored.

**At their best: **Shiba Inus are alert, strong-willed, and confident.

**At their worst: **Shiba Inus are territorial, stubborn, and aggressive.

When suspicious of strangers, they tend to guard and even display territorial [behavior](https://www.thedoghouseps.com/canine-emotions-and-how-to-identify-them/). Like a three-year-old child—for example—they’ll refuse to share toys.
